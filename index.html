Mercury is the smallest planet in the solar system and the closest planet to the sun. It is named after the Roman messenger god, Mercury. Mercury's surface is heavily cratered and has many cliffs and escarpments, indicating that it has experienced significant geological activity in the past. It has a thin atmosphere that is constantly blasted away by the solar wind, leaving the planet's surface exposed to the harsh space environment.

Despite its proximity to the sun, temperatures on Mercury can be extremely cold due to its lack of atmosphere to trap heat.

The planet has been visited by only two spacecraft, Mariner 10 in 1974-75 and MESSENGER from 2011-15. The data returned from these missions has greatly increased our understanding of this fascinating planet.
